How your mouth bacteria can harm your lungs

When gum disease and advanced periodontal disease start to break down oral tissues, they allow oral bacteria to leak into the bloodstream and eventually access the lungs. This breakdown takes place as a result of about 500 microorganisms in the mouth that weaken the gums and underlying bone that normally block these germs from traveling.

Why you should avoid sipping your drinks

A new review uncovers that it is not just what we eat and drink that can affect tooth erosion, but how we eat and drink. Tooth erosion — also known as dental erosion or acid erosion — occurs when acids wear away tooth enamel, which is the substance that coats the outer layer of each tooth. Over time, this erosion could give rise to tooth discoloration, sensitivity, and even tooth loss.
